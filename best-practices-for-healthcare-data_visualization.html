Best Practices for Healthcare Data Visualization

In today’s health care world, where increased regulations and pay for performance models are the order of the day, revenue cycle optimization is critical for success. The revenue cycle is a complex system that provides, hospitals, several opportunities for creating efficiency and cost savings.

  • i) Many organisations still forgo ameliorating the revenue cycle and instead concentrate primarily on cost cutting.
  • ii) Of course spending time on revenue cycle should not replace your focus on expenses, but it’s really important to incorporate efficient healthcare revenue cycle management. In simple terms it means getting paid in a timely manner for services and treatments that you dispense to your patients.
  • iii) It can be challenging to monitor patients’ payments right from the time you accept their appointment till the time their balance is nil.

Let’s run you through some of the best practices for maximum healthcare revenue cycle optimization

Track and Benchmark Your Data


  • i) As they say you can’t measure what you don’t manage. This holds true with healthcare data as well.
  • ii) If you start trending and benchmarking your data, you’ll be able to identify areas to improve your revenue cycle.
  • iii) Once you have identified the areas that need improvement, and the road blocks in achieving that, you can move ahead and find solutions.

Integrate technology


  • i) It’s important to ensure that all the systems that a hospital uses, are integrated for a systematic approach to revenue optimization.
  • ii) Even the most sophisticated software may not benefit hospitals if there are different systems that are not integrated.
  • iii)Also it’s important to not look towards purchasing many pieces of software. Instead invest in a robust solution.

Hire and retain competent staff


  • i) Hiring proficient receptionists and registration personnel who can maintain caring touch points with patients go a long way in helping the revenue cycle.
  • ii) Every staff member plays an active role in the practice’s revenue cycle-from the front staff who informs patients of the payment policies, to the providers and clinical staff who document charges, to the billing office that completes the billing cycle.
  • iii)Educate your staff members on how they personally affect the practice’s revenue cycle in their daily jobs.

Upgrade your tracking


  • i) You should have technology in place to enable proper tracking of the services a patient receives.
  • ii) Also ensure that the charges associated with these services are accurate. This is an important step to realizing an efficient revenue cycle.

Buck up your billing


  • i) There are often time delays between discharging patient and sending the bill to the payers. If so is the case, it presents a good opportunity to the hospital’s billing services to increase efficiency.
  • ii) Make use of a host of new tech solutions to speed up your billing process and get the revenue rotating.
  • iii)Also special attention should be given to speed up collection of payments from both the patients and the insurance companies by removing blocks that arise out of different no. of people and systems allowed.
  • iv) Technology can also enable off-hours payments to be processed more accurately and rapidly through electronic collections while giving patients the convenience of making the payments on the go.

Ultimately every hospital needs to ensure that its revenue cycle is getting the appropriate reimbursement for all the clinical services rendered. Also a hospital needs to annually assess its revenue cycle so as to make sure that revenue capturing is getting as much attention as cost cutting is.
